Banana Peanut Butter Cookies Recipe

Description

These banana peanut butter cookies are extra soft cookies that are perfect to use your overripe bananas. They are full of bananas, cinnamon, peanut butter and mini chocolate chips. They are super chewy and easy to make cookies!

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups All-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 2 tsp Ground cinnamon
  • 2 Overripe mashed bananas
  • 1 cup Unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 1/4 cup Brown sugar, packed light or dark
  • 1/4 cup White granulated sugar
  • 1 cup Peanut butter, creamy
  • 1 Large egg, room temperature
  • 1 tsp Pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups Mini chocolate chips, Plus more to top the cookies

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, sift the flour. Then, add the baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Set aside. Mash the over ripe bananas on a plate using a fork or potato masher. 2 1/2 cups All-purpose flour,1 tsp Baking soda,1 tsp Salt,2 tsp Ground cinnamon,2 Overripe mashed bananas
  2. In a large bowl mix the butter (it should be melted and cooled to room temperature), brown sugar, sugar, peanut butter, mashed bananas, egg, and vanilla. 1 cup Unsalted butter,1 1/4 cup Brown sugar,1/4 cup White granulated sugar,1 cup Peanut butter,1 Large egg,1 tsp Pure vanilla extract
  3. Add the dry ingredients and use a rubber spatula to fold it in. Add the mini chocolate chips and mix. 1 1/2 cups Mini chocolate chips
  4. Preheat the oven to 350Â°F Line two cookie sheets with parchment paper. Let the dough rest while the oven preheats. The gluten gets absorbed into the dough.
  5. Use a 2oz cookie scoop to scoop the dough. Scoop 6 cookie dough balls per cookie sheet. Top with extra mini chocolate chips before baking.
  6. Bake one cookie sheet over at a time. Bake for 13-15 minutes. Bake until the edges are lightly golden and the middles are slightly underbaked. Top with extra mini chocolate chips.
  7. Let the cookies sit on the hot pan for 10 minutes. They are a little fragile while warm. Let them cool completely on a cooling rack.
